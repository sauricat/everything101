# Questions for Thought

1. Take a moment to just feel the texture of what is in front of you. What does your body notice about the resistance or fragility of these materials?
2. How does it feel to physically take control of these objects—to tear them, fold them, and force them into a new meaning?
3. We are cutting paper and cloths today, but how often do we cut ourselves? When we try to navigate censorship or get published, how much of us do we trim away? What does that compromise do to us emotionally?
4. How can this simple, repetitive act of making something with our hands help us take our power back? Can this physical labour be a way to heal from the repressions we face every day, and act as a form of everyday resistance?
