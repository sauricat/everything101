
# Cut, Stitch, Resist! A Visual and Creative Workshop for Gender and Politics Scholars in Times of Precarity

Presented at AAS 2026 Annual Conference, Vancouver Convention Centre, Room 303, 2026-03-13 19:00-20:30

Chair: Yunyun Zhou, Queen Mary University

Presenters:
- Kimberley Manning, Concordia University
- Yunyun Zhou, Queen Mary University
- Yvette-Yuefan Wu, University of Toronto

Amid a global backlash against gender and sexuality studies, this workshop invites scholars working on gender and politics to engage in a collective act of reflection, resistance, and creativity. Across contexts—from increased surveillance and censorship, to shrinking research funding and field access, to precarity and threats faced by international scholars—the academic community is under strain. This workshop aims to collectivize and make visible our lived experiences of marginalization and resilience.

“Cut, Stitch, Resist!” is a participatory, art-based workshop grounded in feminist, queer, and decolonial research practices. Drawing from feminist teachings of visual activism and embodied knowledge, it will introduce participants to creative methods such as zine-making, collage, and textile work. These methods allow researchers to express complex intellectual and affective dimensions of their work beyond text-based formats. Workshop facilitators will share examples from their own creative-political engagements and invite participants to reflect on "disappeared" voices and their own modes of everyday resistance.

Participants will work in small groups to co-create visual pieces that explore cross-border solidarity, institutional exclusion, and feminist care. Materials (paper, thread, fabric, glue, etc.) will be provided. The workshop concludes with a collaborative reflection and sharing session. While connected to broader programming on gender and sexuality in China, the session is open to all scholars working on gender, feminist, and queer politics, particularly in and across the Global South and East.

Learning Objectives:
- Upon completion, differently positioned participants will have forged new connections across geographic, disciplinary, and institutional lines, and generated new possibilities for scholarly solidarity and mutual support.
- Upon completion, participants will be able to implement arts-based methods as part of their research and/or scholarly activism.
